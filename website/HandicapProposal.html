SG@SG Tournament Handicap Audit

Proposed Methodology for Fairer Competition

The SG@SG monthly golf tournament has grown remarkably from a small Saturday gathering to a popular event attracting over 40 players, with strong support from the club’s Director of Golf and Head Pro. The shotgun start format allows the field to finish together, followed by payouts and social time at Copper & Rye, fostering a vibrant community atmosphere.

However, as participation has increased, concerns about handicap fairness have emerged—particularly around the honor-based self-reporting of scores into the GHIN system, which forms the basis of official USGA Handicap Indexes.

To address perceived inequities while maintaining the spirit of the game, the admin team is exploring a tournament-specific handicap adjustment. The goal is to create a more level playing field for SG@SG events by deriving an “Implied Index” based solely on actual performance in our tournaments, rather than relying entirely on external GHIN postings.

Methodology Overview

The proposed approach recalculates each player’s handicap using only their gross scores from SG@SG tournaments (limited to the last 13 months):

  1. Identify Best Scores: For each player, identify their best (lowest) gross scores in the group’s events.
  2. Compute a “Tournament Implied Index” in two variants:
    • Best 3 Analysis: Average the player’s 3 lowest gross scores, then convert that average to a Handicap Index using the course’s standard rating and slope (mirroring the differential formula but based on tournament performance).
    • Best 6 Analysis: Average the player’s 6 lowest gross scores for the same calculation (used when players have sufficient rounds for a more stable sample).
  3. Compare Indices: Compare the Implied Index to the player’s current GHIN Handicap Index.
  4. Calculate Adjustment: Calculate a Suggested Adjustment = Implied Index – Current Index.
    • Negative adjustments indicate players performing significantly better in SG@SG events than their official index suggests (potential “tournament sharpening” or under-posting elsewhere).
    • Positive or zero adjustments indicate alignment or over-performance relative to their GHIN index.

Interactive Dashboard

This data is visualized in an interactive dashboard that displays:

  • Total players and group average gross score
  • Top 3 largest suggested adjustments
  • A sortable, filterable table showing each player’s Implied Index, Current Index, Suggested Adjustment, average gross/net scores, rounds played, and notes (e.g., low round count warnings)

The dashboard allows toggling between Best 3 and Best 6 modes, searching players, and filtering by minimum rounds played, enabling transparent discussion within the group.

This tournament-specific adjustment would only apply to SG@SG events and aims to reward consistent performance in our competitive setting while gently addressing discrepancies. It preserves the honor system but supplements it with empirical data from our own field, promoting fairness as the event continues to grow.
